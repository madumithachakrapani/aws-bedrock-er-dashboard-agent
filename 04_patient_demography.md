# ER Patient Demography – Knowledge Base

## Race Demographics

### Patient Volume by Race

| Race | % of ER Visits |
|---|---|
| White | 37.56% |
| African American | 21.37% |
| Two or More Races | 16.84% |
| Asian | 11.35% |
| Declined to Identify | 11.19% |

| Native American/Alaska Native | 5.43% |

- **White patients** drive the highest ER volume at 37.56%.
- African American patients are the second largest group at 21.37%.

- White patients have the shortest average wait time (37.34 min).

## Gender Demographics

### Key Gender Insights

- Patient volume is **evenly distributed** across gender.
- Wait times are **nearly identical** (~37.7 minutes for both), indicating no gender-based delay.
- **Male patients report higher satisfaction** (6.16) than female patients (5.86).
- Admission rates are similar across gender (~42%), suggesting satisfaction differences are **experience-driven**, not care-level driven.
- **General Practice** shows the highest wait for both genders (capacity bottleneck).
- **Afternoon (12–6 PM)** is peak visit time for both genders.
